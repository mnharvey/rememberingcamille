Her Story

A Life Beautifully Lived

Camille Noe Borgia entered this world on February 2, 1993, and for 33 years she filled it with warmth, laughter, and a love that was impossible to contain. She was a wife, a mother to precious Luca, a daughter, a friend, and a light to everyone fortunate enough to know her.

Those who knew Camille speak of the same things: her spirit, her smile, her generous love for all. Whether it was through her time as a missionary — visiting homes and leaving pieces of her heart in every one — or spinning on the dance floor during country swing nights, or simply being present with the people she loved, Camille gave freely and completely.

She carried her faith quietly and beautifully. She made people feel seen. She made people feel loved. As one friend said, "I've learned that people will forget what you said and what you did, but they'll never forget how you made them feel." Camille made everyone feel that they mattered.

She leaves behind her husband Chase, her son Luca, and a community of family and friends who are better for having known her. She was 33 years old — a life far too short, but a love that will last forever.

"Grief is really just love. It's all the love you want to give, but cannot. All of that unspent love gathers in the corners of your eyes, the lump in your throat, and in the hollow part of your chest. Grief is just love with no place to go."

— Jamie Anderson
